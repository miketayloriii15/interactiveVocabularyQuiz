Frequently Asked Questions

1. What English level are these words suited for?

These words are generally aimed at B2 (upper-intermediate) English levels and higher. They are commonly used vocabulary that you’re likely to encounter in the United States.

2. Do I need to memorize these words?

The goal isn’t necessarily to memorize these words, but rather to understand them and how they are used in context.

3. How should I study these words?

Language learning can be challenging, and everyone learns at their own pace. To build fluency, it’s essential to study regularly and immerse yourself in the language as much as possible.

4. Can I use these words in any English-speaking country?

At All American School of English, we focus on American English as it’s spoken in the United States. While many of these words are used in other English-speaking countries, please be aware that spelling, vocabulary, grammar, punctuation, and pronunciation may differ.

5. How can I sound more like a native speaker from the United States?

You can work on pronunciation, intonation, and stress patterns, expand your vocabulary, practice with native speakers, use common phrases, and familiarize yourself with regional accents and dialects.
